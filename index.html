Mental Help - Mental Health Information


According to Mental Health UK, 1 in every 4 people in the UK has experienced a mental health condition.

This site does not offer diagnosis or treatment, but we do aim to provide helpful information for those living with a mental health problem and those concerned about another person's mental health. We hope you find the information here helpful.

Signs of Poor Mental Health

If someone you know has a mental health issue, they may tell you. If they don't, these signs may indicate that something is wrong:

  • They seem anxious or irritable
  • They are experiencing mood swings
  • They seem withdrawn
  • They have begun to have problems with concentration / memory
  • They are struggling to cope with work or studies

Common Mental Health Conditions

Depression

Low mood and feelings of sadness and / or hopelessness, that may last for weeks or months.

Anxiety

Feelings of fear or worry that can be difficult to control and impact negatively on daily life.

Obsessive Compulsive Disorder (OCD)

A condition causing obsessive, intrusive thoughts and compulsive behaviour.

Bipolar Disorder

A condition characterised by extreme mood swings from high to low and vice versa.

Resources

Here are some resources that may be helpful if you or someone you know experience mental health problems:

  • Mind - Mind provide information and advice on mental health issues.
  • Rethink Mental Illness - A charity that seeks to improve the lives of mentally ill people.
  • Samaritans - A charity aimed at providing support to anyone in emotional distress.
  • Mental Health UK - MHUK deliver national and local services that enable people to understand and manage their mental health.
  • BMHS - An organisation offering mental health support to the BAME community.
